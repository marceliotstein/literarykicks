Literary Kicks



About Us

Literary Kicks was founded in July 1994 by Levi Asher, then a struggling writer bored by his tech job at the headquarters of the JP Morgan bank on Wall Street. Operated surreptitiously from Asher's cubicle (as he pretended to work on PowerPoint presentations), LitKicks quickly became a popular online destination and critic's favorite, also gaining wide usage on college campuses around the world.

In 1996, LitKicks began hosting poetry readings in New York City. In 1998 a separate division, LitKicks Publishing, was formed. The first LitKicks publication was a digital video, Notes From Underground, which was widely praised in Wired Magazine, Entertainment Weekly, Village Voice, New York Press and Time Digital. Boards, Workshops, and a 24-Hour Poetry Party...

LitKicks hosted a number of message boards from January 2001 to July 2004. These boards were available for various discussions on everything from books to politics to members' own creative work. While no longer open for posting, the archives are available here. Selected poems, stories and postings from these boards appear in the new anthology of LitKicks writings, Action Poetry.

This book also features selections from other exciting LitKicks events. In October 2003, LitKicks produced The QUEST, a writing experience that was equal parts workshop and American Idol. Over 100 writers from various countries participated in weekly challenges, and we selected Six Writers to Watch, the contest winners.

In November and December of 2003, The Freestyle Writing Workshop offered a relaxed, focused place for writers to work on different aspects of their craft.

To celebrate our 10th anniversary, LitKicks hosted a 24 HOUR POETRY PARTY, the final result of which is a collaborative poem by 99 writers that chronicles a single day in the life of the earth.

Tech Notes

LitKicks runs on a Linux server with a MySQL database. The content management system was custom-built using Java and JSP. Message boards use the Jive software package, and the search engine is an implementation of Lucene (part of the Jakarta open source project).
