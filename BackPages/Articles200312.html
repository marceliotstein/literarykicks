Remember the books you read when you were a kid? The authors who stretched your imagination? The stories that made you laugh, made you cry, made you think, made you see? Did you have a favorite book or author when you were little? A story or a storyteller that made you love words? Thinking of something? Good.

LitKicks is seeking to add a new genre to its articles section, and so we're announcing our first-ever targeted articles drive. What does this mean? Well, we'd like to add children's literature to our articles lineup. So, for the next two months, we'd like you to submit articles on children's authors and books. From Dr. Seuss to C.S. Lewis, Charlie and the Chocolate Factory to Are You There, God? It's Me, Margaret, there's plenty to choose from. If you've never written a LitKicks article, now is the perfect time to give it a shot. Pick a favorite and go for it.

Or perhaps tripping down memory lane to the books you read in your childhood doesn't interest you. Maybe you're more interested in work by today's writers: Chuck Palahniuk, Irvine Welsh, Dave Eggers, David Foster Wallace. We're seeking to expand our Postmodern Transgressive genre, and would like author bios and book reviews. If you've read a book that fits into this category and have an opinion on it (good or bad), write about it! All points of view are welcome, so share your thoughts!

How To Contribute An Article

We are looking for articles on any literary subject. Please check out some of the contributions on this page before proceeding to submit your own article, and please read the LitKicks Style Guide carefully. Your article will be edited and may be cut before it appears elsewhere on the site. And, contrary to popular belief, spelling actually does count.

We are especially looking for author biographies or book reviews. If you would like to post comments on articles, or ask questions to authors of LitKicks articles, please use the Writers & Genres discussion board rather than the article template. Please check out the list of recent articles, or just go ahead and create your own article now.
