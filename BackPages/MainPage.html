Litkicks has been around since July 23, 1994, and we're still here. We never had a business model, which is why we don't have to go out of business now.

The site is devoted to a few experimental literary movements that tried to uncover some deeper truths about life. In studying the life stories of the writers as well as their works, there are sometimes even more interesting truths to be revealed than are found in the works themselves. We at Literary Kicks believe in deconstructionism as long as you clean up after you're done. And we do not believe masterpieces exist, nor do we want them to. We prefer the glory of brilliant mistakes.

There are also a few entries that have yet to be categorized (and hopefully will be soon) on Vladimir Mayakovsky, Ralph Ellison, the Harlem Renaissance, Basho, J. D. Salinger, Henry Miller and Jean Genet.

Each of the main areas above have their own discussion boards which are useful for asking questions, exploring ideas or just "shooting the shit". For completely free-form discussions, irrational inquiries or other kinds of random weirdness, try the general discussion board.

There are also boards for road stories and for interesting real life experiences with notable writers. If you are a writer, please try out your poetry, spontaneous poetry or fiction. Most importantly, we are now trying to accept article contributions from anyone with knowledge or thoughts to share: visit this page to get started.

LitKicks has always been mainly the personal creation of Levi Asher, but he's kind of sick of it all and just wants to go home and listen to some CD's for a while. He's still around, but hopefully will blend more and more into the wallpaper each day.

If you miss some of the stuff that used to be here, visit the Back Pages.
