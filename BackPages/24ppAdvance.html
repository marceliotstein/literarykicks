Latest News: We have shut down the LitKicks boards in order to begin the transformation of the entire site into the 24 HOUR POETRY PARTY. We can't wait to get started and we really hope you'll be a part of this experiment.

If you'd like to prepare ahead of time, we'd like to ask you to practice working with rhyme and meter. Many of the activities we are planning for this party will involve contributing poetic fragments according to a specific request (for example, we may ask you to write lines containing exactly seven syllables that rhyme with 'rain' or 'door'). To best prepare yourself for this creative process, we'd like to ask you to find and read some excellent poetry today, and pay special attention to the rhythms and textures a good poet employs.

Important note: even if you have never written a line of poetry before, we hope you will contribute to this event. If you can talk and think, you have all the qualifications a writer needs. If you haven't created a LitKicks membername yet, you will need one to participate -- start here.

Finally, if you're anywhere near New York City, please join us for a casual afterparty party and open mic at the Bowery Poetry Club this Saturday from 2 to 4 pm, where we'll group-read our newly invented 24 hour poem, play some music, have some fun and complain about lack of sleep.

No matter who you are or where you are, we want you to be part of this party! See you inside.

LitKicks Presents ...
friday, july 23 12 noon to saturday july 24 12 noon EST
The 24 HOUR POETRY PARTY is one of the most ambitious poetry experiments ever attempted. The entire event will take place online at LitKicks.com during the course of a single day and night. Seeded with original poems, writings and koans by a number of renowned poets, participants all over the world will join in the spontaneous composition of a single epic "real-time poem" describing 24 hours in the life of planet Earth.

Please join us at LitKicks.com between July 23 12 noon and July 24 12 noon EST to stretch your poetic skills, celebrate the 10th anniversary of the world's best literary website, and create poetry history.

  • Who is invited to participate?
    You and all your friends.

  • Where do we go?
    Go to LitKicks.com at any time between Friday, July 23 2004 12 noon and Saturday, July 24 2004 12 noon.

  • Who are the special guests?
    We've asked a few notable poets, writers and interesting people to contribute to this event. We're proud to have new work by Robert Creeley, Michael McClure, John Cassady, Bob Holman, David Amram, Charles Plymell, George Wallace, Gary "Mex" Glezner, Sander Hicks, Gary Gach and Eliot Katz, along with litero-electronica sonic backdrop by DJ Spooky also featuring cuts from Michael McClure and Ray Manzarek.

  • Is writing experience necessary?
    LitKicks does not believe in writing experience. If you can talk you can write.

  • What do I need to bring?
    Your brain. High speed connection preferred.
