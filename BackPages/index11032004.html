Shell-Shocked and Awed

After watching too much television news last night, I got up early this morning to check the latest election results, hopeful that there would be some definitive answer as to who came out victorious.

The answer isn't definitive yet, but all signs seem to point toward victory for incumbent President Bush.

Last night, as I volunteered with MoveOn PAC for a last-ditch, election-night get out the vote effort, I felt something verging on euphoria. Things were looking good. The voters we were talking to were voting. And I knew that in battleground states like mine, there were hundreds of people out doing the same thing and, I hoped, getting the same results.

It's been inspiring to stand behind someone I believed in (it's no secret -- I voted Kerry) and do what I could to help. And no matter how the final counts turn out, I'll continue being glad that I volunteered. But, speaking from my own experience, all of that optimism and hope poured into that one direction made me feel that the optimism and hope was enough to make things go the way I wanted them to.

Of course, that's naive. And I know that. But I also want to point out if things end up going the way they look like they will, and it's a loss for the guy I'm rooting for, it doesn't have to be a loss forever. So many people worked so hard together because they saw a need to do so. No matter who gets sworn in in January, the need isn't going to go away, and I hope that sense of camaraderie I've felt sticks around. As I'm constantly reminded, I'm very young, but I do still believe that community can change things.

And that's the end of my mostly-partisan thoughts this morning. Share yours.
