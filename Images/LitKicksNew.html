Turn off your mind relax and float downstream.

This is where experimental, beat and alternative literature gets kicked around. Literary Kicks has been on the web for a long, long time -- since July 23, 1994 to be exact -- and since we've already won the record for most years without a redesign, we are *finally* changing the site around. The main improvement is to allow contributions, comments and various other interactions from members. After six and a half year as a monologue, LitKicks is turning itself into an open mic.

The core of the site remains what it always was: a set of biographical pages on a few Beat authors:

Jack Kerouac

Allen Ginsberg

As well as Neal Cassady, William S. Burroughs, Gary Snyder, Lawrence Ferlinghetti, Gregory Corso or Michael McClure. There are also pages on The Beat Generation, Buddhism: The Beat Religion, Jazz and the Beats, the origin of the term 'Beat', Beat Connections in Music, Beat Movies and Beat Moments on TV.

For free-form discussions, term-paper questions, rational inquiry or random weirdness, try the general discussion board. There are boards for road stories and for interesting real life experiences with notable writers. If you've written any fiction or poetry you'd like to get a reaction to, you can try it out here. Most importantly, we are now trying to accept article contributions from anyone with knowledge or thoughts to share: visit this page to get started.

LitKicks has always been mainly the personal creation of Levi Asher, but he's kind of sick of it all and just wants to go home and listen to some CD's for a while. He's still around, but hopefully will blend more and more into the wallpaper each day.

If you miss some of the stuff that used to be here, visit the Back Pages. The image at the top of this page is an alteration of a picture of Paul Verlaine, the 19th Century French poet. The quote under it (turn off your mind ...) is from John Lennon.
