The Summer of the Mets is a story about two troubled 15-year-olds who try to fall in love.

Lianne Fahey is a tough-talking small town girl who expresses herself sometimes in art, and sometimes in sudden acts of self-destructive behavior. She changes her clothes and eye color several times a day and her mind nearly as often, and seems to fear nothing but the possibility of her own boredom.

Chris Blomberg suffers from pathological shyness, and lives in complete social isolation in his upscale Long Island town. Victimized for years by a cruel best friend, he finally breaks away and ventures out into the world, only to fall blindly into the confusing whirlpool of first-time love.

The setting is suburban America, mid-1980's. Ronald Reagan is in the White House, Sammy Hagar is in Van Halen, and the New York Mets are in the World Series. In the midst of it all, two lonely kids are reaching out to each other and trying not to get too badly hurt.
