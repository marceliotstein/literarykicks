Literary Kicks

Join the LitKicks Poetry Karaoke Team at the Bowery Poetry Club in New York City, Feb 17 9:30 pm (or just come to watch). We're going to be competing in the KPF series, a wild-and-crazy, long-running event hosted by poets Daniel Nester and Regie Cabico. Each participant will read one poem and sing one song. We need six members for the LitKicks team, and we still have one opening.

We will be up against some strong competition: the Painted Bride Quarterly from New Jersey. See what happens when the editors and writers from an acclaimed journal go up against the world's best literary website. If you would like to perform, please email Levi Asher at brooklyn@litkicks.com. If you don't want to perform, please come by just to cheer us on. More info here: KPF AT THE BOWERY!


A few LitKicks writers, including Andrew Lundwall, Firecracker, Levi Asher, Doreen Peri and Lightning Rod, will be at the Poetic Inhalation poetry reading on Jan 29 in Arlington, Virginia (near Washington D.C.) More info here.

Live poetry readings are an important part of LitKicks. We bring together poets and fiction writers who post to our boards as well as special guests and visiting musicians. We've been honored to hear from some really amazing personalities at past LitKicks readings, such as David Amram, Bob Holman, Richard Hell, Lee Ranaldo, Nicole Blackman, John S. Hall, the Washington Squares, Todd Colby, Sander Hicks and (in his live performance debut) John Cassady. Many LitKicks poets have also performed at our readings, which are open to beginners as well as experienced performers.

Our favorite place to do poetry readings is the Bowery Poetry Club in New York City because it's just a real good place (and NY City is pretty much our home base). The Eclectic Gallery, a friendly art gallery in Battle Creek, Michigan, is our #1 home away from home. We hope to be doing more locations in the near future, including our first shows in the South and in California.
