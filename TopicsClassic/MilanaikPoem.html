THAT CLASSIC JAZZ

by David Milanaik
milanaik@princeton.edu

sweet keys being stroked into
song rolling like blood from a
small wound easy, slow and
thick, rich like a dab of fudge
mellow as red wine

the voice down and up like
crests of white cap rising
to pat the edge of a docked
boat and sinking to spring
again in a controlled tremble
easing into a breadth of silence
and back again like the face
of an old lover longed to be
seen again
