Hairspray

This was one of John Waters' more accessible films, and I thought it was very good. It's about a spunky, unconventional and relentlessly cheerful teenage girl who breaks social and racial barriers in her pursuit of dancing nirvana in the early 60's. The girl was played by Ricki Lake, who is now a popular talk show host, but was then an unknown actress. The best bit of casting was Debbie Harry and Sonny Bono as the stressed-out parents of a rival teenager.

This is beat-related because of the scene in which four "squares" wander into a beatnik's house ... when the beatnik begins reading 'Howl' the squares run away. Thanks to Ahmad Nimeh (animeh@gopher.chem.wayne.edu) for reminding me of this scene (which is similar, interestingly, to a scene in the film 'American Pop').
