1 pm

orbital happiness
stellar gorgeousness, a cosmic chain
constellations
and a puddle
a puddle of rain
too rough from my hands, from other than me
no grace. nothing. nothing unfolds as it should
and it should be
i felt something like pain,
sliding out of me,
aching in my chair
meandering, not melancholy
upended books, unopened bills,
unbidden offers sent to me,
a watch face down, a glossy photo,
no space left free
a neglected glass, dusty typewriter at an open door
an empty duffle tossed upon a hardwood floor
i feel a growling beat
i am but a stain
groping for
me
(and the rain
and the door
and a sea in a tree)
from where i sit
the view does not inspire --
papers stacked on desk, tables and floor
little time for reverie
or introspection
the job requires one to see
what is there
not to dream what could be

3pm writing challenge

In the spirit of this poem by Bob Holman, we'd like you to write about somebody you miss, or somebody you wish to remember.

Please try to write ONLY in lines of eight to fourteen syllables. This is essential for the rhythm we are trying to establish for this section of the poem. Please pay attention to the meter of your lines to make sure they flow smoothly. For example, Shakespeare's phrase:

"a tale told by an idiot, full of sound and fury, signifying nothing"

is a smooth series of "iambs", or matched syllables. A different type of rhythm (using "trochees", which are backwards iambs) is established when Gary Snyder writes:

"Cedar planking, wooden bucket, water-splashing"

Please give us long lines (no less than eight syllables per line) that flow simply and easily.
