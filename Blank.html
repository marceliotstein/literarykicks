About LitKicks

LitKicks was founded in July 1994 by Levi Asher, then a struggling writer bored by his tech job at the headquarters of the JP Morgan bank. Operated surreptitiously from Asher's cubicle on Wall Street, LitKicks quickly became a popular online destination and "critic's favorite," also gaining wide usage on college campuses around the world.

In 1996, LitKicks began hosting poetry readings in New York City. In 1998 a separate division, LitKicks Publishing, was formed. The first LitKicks publication was a digital video, "Notes From Underground", which was widely praised in Wired Magazine, Entertainment Weekly, Village Voice, New York Press and Time Digital. LitKicks has grown to include several volunteer staffers and plans to increase its offline presence with more live events, video projects and book publications in 2003.

    Tech Notes

    LitKicks runs on a Linux server with a MySQL database. The content management system was custom-built in Java/JSP. Message boards use the Jive software package, and the search engine is an implementation of Lucene (part of the Jakarta open source project. Graphic design takes place on the tangerine iMac in Levi Asher's apartment in Queens.
