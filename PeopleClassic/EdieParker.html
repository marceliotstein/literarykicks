Edie Parker

Edie Parker, a wild party girl from upper-class Grosse Pointe, Michigan, fell in with Lucien Carr, Jack Kerouac and that whole Columbia University crowd when they were students there in the early forties. She was close friends with Joan Vollmer Adams, later the common-law wife (and shooting victim) of William S. Burroughs, and the two young women kept an off-campus apartment together where much early Beat history took place.

She became Edie Kerouac briefly, and took a confused young Jack back to Michigan with her for a few months before he split for good. They had no children together. Edie would go on to write her memoirs under the the name Frankie Edith Kerouac Parker.
