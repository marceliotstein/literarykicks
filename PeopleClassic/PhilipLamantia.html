Philip Lamantia

Born: October 23, 1927
Place of Birth: San Francisco

Philip Lamantia discovered Surrealism as a teenager in San Francisco. Immediately drawn to this movement, he began to write Surrealist poetry and left California for New York to meet Andre Breton, who immediately recognized the young man's talent and began publishing his poems. Lamantia's work appeared in Breton's VVV, as well as Charles Henri Ford's View and other experimental journals.

While in New York, Lamantia became friends with Carl Solomon and Allen Ginsberg. and several other members of the crowd that would later become the Beat movement. Lamantia was the first to read at the Six Gallery poetry reading in 1955. Instead of reading his own works that night, he read several poems by a friend, John Hoffman, who'd recently died in Mexico, apparently of a peyote overdose.

Now classified, however incorrectly, as a Beat poet, Lamantia published several popular books of poetry, and has remained active in avant-garde circles.
